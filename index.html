Italian-Lasagna

We may never know the exact person who created lasagna.
In fact, this dish was not named after an individual, but after a fermented noodle called laganon that was frequently used in ancient Greece where lasagna originated. In the beginning, lasagna was sprinkled with toppings, and eaten with a pointed stick. Unrecognizable by today’s standards, the only similarity between the two was the layering of pasta and sauce...

Types of lasagna

1) Fresh Spinach Ravioli Lasagna


Fresh Spinach Ravioli blew my mind. I feel as though it falls under vegetarian category because we all know lasagna is quite meaty. This looks so fresh and new try this creative recipe that uses fresh spinach filled ravioli as the pasta layer in a delightful lasagna dish. Spinach ravioli combine with ricotta cheese, artichoke hearts and a creamy spinach sauce in a lasagna dish that's baked, then browned on top for extra taste and appeal.


2) Lobster Lasagna


This delicious dish is definitely for people who enjoy sea food & want to try something new with the lobster, instead of eating it with just melted butter.
One could make lasagna with the lobster, the whole concept sounds divine & really classy.
Lobster is a luxury food that makes the usual lasagna recipe suddenly special and elegant enough to serve to the most demanding guests.


3) Spicy Chicken Lasagna

This is more for people who enjoy chicken more than beef. As much as beef lasagna is classy ,I think chicken lasagna is a classic dish.
It accommodates people who love chicken and it is very diverse, The chicken is usually spicy to bring out the flavours in the lasagna to make it tasty. The chicken is shradded just like the cheese, tomato sauce is added instead of cheese sauce. It is very delicious and unique & it is worthy to try out at family events.


4) Vegetarian Pumpkin Lasagna


This delightful dish caught me off guard it is quite interesting and a MUST try in my list. Pumpkin is quite sweet & I have never had a sweet pumpkin lasagna so this is quite interesting. This vegetarian-pumpkin-lasagna has layers of noodles and instead of meat it has pumpkin and pumpkin sauce. I want to try this as in yesterday!! , it's a different concept amongst them all and looks very yummy...

1.Fresh Spinach Ravioli Lasagna Recipe

  • prep: 5mins
  • Cook: 30mins
  • Total: 35mins
  • 6 Servings

Ingridients

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups coarsely chopped fresh spinach
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • Freshly grated nutmeg, to taste, optional kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 1 1/2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 3 (9-ounce) packages spinach-artichoke ravioli, or similar
  • 1 (6-ounce) can artichoke hearts, drained and coarsely chopped

Directions...

  • 01 Preheat the oven to 375 F. Spray a 9 x 9-inch square pan, or another 1 1/2- to 2-quart baking dish, with nonstick cooking spray.

  • 02 Bring a large pot of water to a boil for pasta.

  • 03 In a large deep saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for one minute until the mixture turns golden. Add the spinach and cook until barely wilted, about 2 minutes, stirring frequently. Gradually stir in the milk, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Allow to simmer for a minute or two, stirring occasionally, until thickened. Season with the nutmeg, if desired, and salt and pepper. Stir in 1/2 cup Parmesan.

  • 04 Meanwhile, combine the ricotta cheese, lemon zest, ​and garlic in a bowl, seasoning with salt and pepper.

  • 05 When the water comes to a boil, season with salt, let it return to a boil, and cook the pasta according to package directions, stopping just before it becomes completely cooked.

  • 06 Spoon just enough creamy spinach sauce in the bottom of a lasagna dish to lightly coat it. Top with half of the ravioli, then top the pasta with a layer of half of the ricotta mixture, and half of the chopped artichokes. Spoon over half of the remaining creamy spinach sauce , then sprinkle over half of the Parmesan. Repeat these layers. Place in the oven and bake for about 15 minutes until everything browns and bubbles. Run the lasagna under the broiler for a final minute to brown the top if desired.

2. Lobster-lasagna Recipe

  • prep: 5mins
  • Cook: 25mins
  • Total: 30mins
  • 12 Servings

Ingridients

  • For the Lobster Filling:
    1/2 pound cooked lobster meat
  • 1 teaspoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup (1.2 ounces) julienned leek (white part only)
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • 2 teaspoons dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup fresh ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup (1.3 ounces) shredded fresh mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup (3.2 ounces) Alfredo sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons tomato paste (doppio concentrato di pomodoro)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)

    • For the Ricotta-Mozzarella-Mascarpone Filling:

  • 3/4 cup (2.6 ounces) shredded fresh mozzarella cheese
  • 1/3 cup (2.8 ounces) fresh ricotta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon (1/2 ounce) mascarpone cheese
  • Pinch fine sea salt

    • For the Lasagna:

  • 1 package (8.8 ounces/250 grams/6 sheets) fresh lasagne sheets
  • 1/4 cup (2 ounces) Alfredo sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup (1.8 ounces) shredded fresh mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)

    • Directions...


    To Make the Lobster Filling

  • 01 Heat the olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat.

  • 02 Add the leek and cook until softened, two to three minutes.

  • 03 Add the garlic and white wine and continue cooking until garlic is softened, another two to three minutes.

  • 04 Remove from heat and let cool.

  • 05 In a large bowl, mix all of the remaining lobster filling ingredients.

  • 07 Then add in the cooled leek-garlic mixture and mix to combine.

  • 08 Set aside.

    • To Make the Ricotta-Mozzarella-Mascarpone Filling
  • 09 Mix all ingredients in a large bowl and set aside.

    • To Assemble and Bake the Lasagna
  • 10 Preheat the oven to 350 F (176 C).

  • 11 Distribute the Alfredo sauce evenly over the bottom of an 11-inch by 7-inch baking dish.

  • 12 Cover the Alfredo sauce with an even layer of lasagna sheets.

  • 13 Spread half of the lobster filling over the pasta sheets.

  • 14 Then cover with the second layer of pasta sheets.

  • 15 Spread the ricotta-mozzarella-mascarpone filling over the second layer of lasagna sheets.

  • 16 Then cover with the third sheet of lasagna.

  • 17 Spread the remaining lobster filling over the final sheet of pasta.

  • 18 Then cover evenly with the shredded fresh mozzarella and tomatoes.

  • 19 Bake for 20 minutes or until lightly browned on top.
